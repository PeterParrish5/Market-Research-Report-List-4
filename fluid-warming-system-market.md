<p><p>Fluid warming systems are medical devices used to raise the temperature of intravenous fluids before entering the patient's body to prevent hypothermia. There are two main types of fluid warming systems: ultrasound and electric. Ultrasound systems use sound waves to heat the fluid rapidly, while electric systems use electronic heating elements. Both types of systems are efficient in maintaining the desired temperature of fluids, ensuring safe and effective delivery to patients during medical procedures.</p></p>
