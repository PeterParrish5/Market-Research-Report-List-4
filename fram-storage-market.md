<p><p>FRAM (Ferroelectric Random Access Memory) storage comes in two main market types: Serial FRAM and Parallel FRAM. Serial FRAM utilizes a serial interface for communication, making it suitable for applications requiring a smaller footprint and lower power consumption. On the other hand, Parallel FRAM uses a parallel interface, enabling faster data transfer rates and higher performance in applications with larger data requirements. Both types of FRAM storage are non-volatile and offer high-speed read and write capabilities, making them ideal for various industrial and automotive applications.</p></p>
