<p><p>Bone cement is used in orthopedic surgeries to fix implants to bones. There are three types of bone cement based on viscosity: low, medium, and high viscosity cements. Low viscosity cements are used for vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty procedures due to their ability to flow easily into small spaces. Medium viscosity cements are commonly used in hip and knee replacements. High viscosity cements are ideal for surgeries involving large bone voids or compromised bone quality, providing better anchorage for implants.</p></p>
