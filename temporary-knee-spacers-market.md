<p><p>Temporary knee spacers are used in patients with infected knee joints to maintain joint space and enable antibiotics to penetrate the infected area. The two main types of knee spacers are cement and metal. Cement spacers are made of bone cement and antibiotics, providing temporary support and releasing medication over time. Metal spacers are typically made of stainless steel or titanium, providing structural support and releasing antibiotics through a drug-eluting mechanism. Both types have advantages and limitations in the treatment of infected knee joints.</p></p>
