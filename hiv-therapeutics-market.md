<p><p>The HIV therapeutics market includes several types of medications: Nucleoside-Analog Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs), Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NNRTIs), Entry and Fusion Inhibitors, Protease Inhibitors (PIs), Integrase Inhibitors, and Coreceptor Antagonists. NRTIs work by blocking the activity of reverse transcriptase, NNRTIs bind to and disable the enzyme, Entry and Fusion Inhibitors block the HIV virus from entering cells, PIs prevent the virus from replicating, Integrase Inhibitors block the enzyme needed for the virus to integrate its genetic material into host cells, and Coreceptor Antagonists block the virus from entering cells by targeting specific receptors.</p></p>
