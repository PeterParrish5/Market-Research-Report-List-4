<p><p>Cell culture media for vaccine production can be broadly categorized into two types: with serum and serum-free. Serum-containing media utilize animal-derived serum to provide essential nutrients and growth factors for cell growth and vaccine production. On the other hand, serum-free media are formulated without any animal-derived components and are often preferred due to issues related to serum variability, ethical concerns, and potential risks of contamination. Both types of media play a critical role in supporting the growth and production of vaccines in cell culture systems.</p></p>
