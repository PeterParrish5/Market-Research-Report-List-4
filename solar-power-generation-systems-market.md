<p><p>Centralized PV power plants are large-scale solar power generation systems where electricity is produced in one location and distributed through the grid. These plants typically have a capacity of over 1 MW and are owned and operated by utility companies or large energy companies. On the other hand, decentralized PV power plants are smaller-scale systems that are installed on rooftops or in local communities. These plants usually have a capacity of less than 1 MW and can be owned by individuals or small businesses.</p></p>
