<p><p>ICU patient monitors are classified into three types based on the level of care they provide: high-acuity monitors, mid-acuity monitors, and low-acuity monitors. </p><p>High-acuity monitors are specifically designed for intensive care units where patients require continuous monitoring of vital signs and parameters. </p><p>Mid-acuity monitors are used in step-down units or progressive care units for patients who do not require constant monitoring but still need regular observation. </p><p>Low-acuity monitors are used in general wards or outpatient settings for patients with stable conditions that only require periodic monitoring.</p></p>
